At FH OÖ Campus Wels | School of Engineering we use #JaamSim and #CoppeliaSim from Coppelia Robotics for teaching the design and simulation of industrial robot applications.

JaamSim implements discrete event simulation semantics and can be used during conceptual design of production processes aiding with the overall factory layout and material flow. Instead, CoppeliaSim implements continuous time simulation semantics and can be used during detailed design of work cells aiding with the equipment layout and motion control.

Both products can be installed and used freely for educational purposes. Consequently, they are a good fit for our distance learning scenarios. However, for commercial purposes different products might be favorable (e.g. AnyLogic, Visual Components, visTABLE, Siemens Plant Simulation, Siemens Process Simulate, or RoboDK)!

One drawback of the tools, which I personally noticed, is that the IT architecture of the system under development cannot be described easily. However, the IT architecture represents a major concern in smart factory scenarios. Consequently, I believe that in the future the design and simulation tools need to be improved!
