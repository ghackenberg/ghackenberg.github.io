In the random routing strategy, for each job the sequence of machines is selected randomly from all possible sequences of machines with a uniform probability distribution. In the sequential scheduling strategy, the jobs are processed strictly one after the other (no interleaving of jobs).

Obviously, this routing and scheduling strategy results in poor machine utilization and throughput. One of our next steps is to implement better routing and scheduling strategies for this class of systems. Ideally, we hope to ship good standard strategies with the software, which can be used to get started quickly with system prototyping. However, we also expect that tailored strategies with dedicated search heuristics are required for achieving near-optimal system performance.
