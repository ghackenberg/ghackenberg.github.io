The k-means algorithm represents a basic tool for any data scientist today. Did you ever wonder what the algorithm does or how it works?

Maybe this visualization helps you understand how k-means finds clusters in datasets (e.g. customers with similar behavior or documents about similar topics). In the first step a predefined number of clusters is distributed randomly across the dataset. Subsequently, the clusters are attracted by the surrounding data points until a stable configuration is found. That's it!

As you might notice, the results of the algorithm heavily depend on the predefined number of clusters as well as the random distribution of the clusters across the dataset in the first step.

Other clustering algorithms exist, which might outperform k-means in certain scenarios. George Seif prepared a good overview: https://lnkd.in/ejwit-4

Visualization prepared for students at FH OÖ Campus Wels | School of Engineering, University of Applied Sciences Upper Austria.
