Following up on my programmatic comic animation, I put together an explainer video highlighting the geometry and math behind the procedural character rig. 

This serves as a visual walkthrough to verify and validate the trigonometry equations and coordinate systems that my coding agent, Antigravity, has come up with:

1️⃣ 𝐀𝐧𝐚𝐥𝐲𝐭𝐢𝐜𝐚𝐥 𝐉𝐨𝐢𝐧𝐭 𝐈𝐊 (𝐋𝐚𝐰 𝐨𝐟 𝐂𝐨𝐬𝐢𝐧𝐞𝐬): Bending knees naturally by analytically solving the triangle formed by the Hip, Knee, and Foot in constant time: cos(alpha) = (d^2 + l1^2 - l2^2) / (2 * d * l1)

2️⃣ 𝐃𝐮𝐚𝐥-𝐏𝐡𝐚𝐬𝐞 𝐆𝐚𝐢𝐭 𝐊𝐢𝐧𝐞𝐦𝐚𝐭𝐢𝐜𝐬: Splitting the gait cycle into a linear stance translation (no foot sliding) and a sinusoidal swing arc.

3️⃣ 𝐅𝐨𝐨𝐭 𝐑𝐨𝐥𝐥𝐢𝐧𝐠 𝐌𝐞𝐜𝐡𝐚𝐧𝐢𝐜𝐬: Tilting the foot dynamically relative to the gait progress to simulate heel strike, rolling stance, and toe-off.

4️⃣ 𝐖𝐚𝐥𝐤𝐢𝐧𝐠 𝐆𝐚𝐢𝐭 (𝐒𝐭𝐚𝐧𝐜𝐞 𝐎𝐯𝐞𝐫𝐥𝐚𝐩): Visualizing how walking maintains continuous ground support through overlapping stance phases (Double Stance).

5️⃣ 𝐑𝐮𝐧𝐧𝐢𝐧𝐠 𝐆𝐚𝐢𝐭 (𝐅𝐥𝐢𝐠𝐡𝐭 𝐏𝐡𝐚𝐬𝐞): Visualizing the stance decay that removes the overlap and introduces an airborne flight phase, lifting the character's hips.

6️⃣ 𝟐𝐃 𝐑𝐢𝐠𝐠𝐢𝐧𝐠 & 𝐏𝐨𝐥𝐲𝐠𝐨𝐧 𝐒𝐤𝐢𝐧𝐧𝐢𝐧𝐠: Binding the low-poly outline meshes to joint pivots, weighting vertices around the joints to deform smoothly.

The visualizations are built in Remotion using React and SVGs. The code is fully open-source and hosted on GitHub. Let me know what you think!
