With CADdrive we are getting closer to automatic 𝐅𝐄𝐌 𝐒𝐢𝐦𝐮𝐥𝐚𝐭𝐢𝐨𝐧 of arbitrary LEGO structures. The FEM models (mostly connected springs, contact faces, boundary constraints, and external forces) are derived from LEGO / LDraw models using our custom 𝐂𝐀𝐃𝐝𝐫𝐢𝐯𝐞 𝐏𝐲𝐭𝐡𝐨𝐧 𝐋𝐢𝐛𝐫𝐚𝐫𝐲.

Our first use case is 𝐁𝐫𝐢𝐝𝐠𝐞 𝐃𝐞𝐬𝐢𝐠𝐧. The idea came because bridge design with LEGO bricks is a classical challenge in corporate project management trainings. (I ran through such trainings myself during my time at Concept Reply GmbH / the Reply AG and enjoyed it much!)

Now we are working on extending the LEGO FEM simulation capabilities (supporting more brick types as well as brick rotation) and integrating the capabilities into the 𝐂𝐀𝐃𝐝𝐫𝐢𝐯𝐞 𝐖𝐞𝐛 𝐈𝐧𝐭𝐞𝐫𝐟𝐚𝐜𝐞 so people of all ages and educational backgrounds can use them easily.

If you would like to try out our LEGO FEM simulation capabilities or start contributing to CADdrive, do not hesitate to text me or my colleague Christian! Also feel free to follow us on GitHub to get more insights into what we are working on and how it works technically.
