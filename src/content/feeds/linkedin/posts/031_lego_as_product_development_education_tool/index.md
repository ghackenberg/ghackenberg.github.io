If you think #LEGO is just a toy, you might want to reconsider your opinion 😉 Personally, I believe LEGO can be an extraordinary tool for 𝐩𝐫𝐨𝐝𝐮𝐜𝐭 𝐝𝐞𝐯𝐞𝐥𝐨𝐩𝐦𝐞𝐧𝐭 and 𝐬𝐲𝐬𝐭𝐞𝐦𝐬 𝐞𝐧𝐠𝐢𝐧𝐞𝐞𝐫𝐢𝐧𝐠 𝐞𝐝𝐮𝐜𝐚𝐭𝐢𝐨𝐧 ... for all ages and at all career levels! Why: Because LEGO lowers entry barriers for people with different educational backgrounds (you don't have to be a CAD expert), while enabling interesting real-world system designs based on a standard component library. The limit is only your creativity ... and of course time.

With CADdrive, we merge the familiar LEGO / #LDraw / #LeoCAD -experience with the awesome GitHub-experience to form a unique 𝐝𝐢𝐠𝐢𝐭𝐚𝐥 𝐥𝐞𝐚𝐫𝐧𝐢𝐧𝐠 𝐞𝐧𝐯𝐢𝐫𝐨𝐧𝐦𝐞𝐧𝐭 including #CAD model versioning (supports branch + merge), issue management, as well as milestone planning and progress tracking. With this platform, people can gain first-hand experience in project management, product design, systems engineering, agile development, digital collaboration, and much more with only little ramp-up friction losses.

The image below shows a paper folding machine developed by a group of four 𝐑𝐨𝐛𝐨𝐭𝐢𝐜 𝐒𝐲𝐬𝐭𝐞𝐦𝐬 𝐄𝐧𝐠𝐢𝐧𝐞𝐞𝐫𝐢𝐧𝐠 (𝐑𝐒𝐄) 𝐌𝐚𝐬𝐭𝐞𝐫 students. The design project also includes a system requirements specification, a mechatronic architecture specification, an electrical/electronic system specification, and a software behavior specification (which is not shown here). I am totally amazed, what my students achieved in this short period of time and what they report on the efficiency and effectiveness of the learning experience!
